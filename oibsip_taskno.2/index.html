Salvador Dalí

Master of Surrealism and Imagination

Biography

Salvador Dalí (1904-1989) was one of the most influential surrealist artists of the 20th century. Known for his flamboyant personality and dream-like, eccentric artwork, he revolutionized the art world with his boundless imagination. His most iconic work, "The Persistence of Memory," features the famous melting clocks that became synonymous with his style.

Famous Works

  • The Persistence of Memory (1931)
  • Swans Reflecting Elephants (1937)
  • The Elephants (1948)
  • Galatea of the Spheres (1952)
  • The Hallucinogenic Toreador (1970)
